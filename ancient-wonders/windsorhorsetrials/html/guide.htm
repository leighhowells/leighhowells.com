Horse Trials, or as it is more popularly known - 'Eventing' - is the fastest growing of all equestrian sports. Great Britain has always been in the forefront of the sport and hosts more Events than any other country. It is therefore no surprise to find many
foreign competitors based more
or less permanently in this country.

The origins of the sport go back to the days when the horse played an active role in military combat. The three phases of the sport, Dressage, Cross Country and Show Jumping represent the necessary disciplines and attributes of a horse obedient and attentive to its rider, yet still possessing sufficient boldness and energy to go at speed across country, over hedges, ditches and other obstacles in pursuit of the enemy.
