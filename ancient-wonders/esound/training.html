Training Centre, Birmingham

This £2 million project involved converting Hollymoor Mental Hospital into a hi-tech multimedia training and community facility. The two-storey hospital was constructed in 1905 and is sent in 240 acres of parkland on Birmingham Great Park.

It has a number of distinct areas, notably the 17,000 sq ft main building and a grade B-listed chapel. The main building now houses a training centre, advice and resource centre, cafeteria, administration and conference halls, while the chapel was converted into a performance hall.

In addition, two extensions were built to the main building. One – housing a crèche – is extensively glazed and has a curved metal roof and canopy; the other is simpler, accommodating a lift and disabled access.

One of the project’s main challenges was the external restoration of the main building. The process for restoring damaged terracotta chimney pots, feature stone work and cornices was quite complicated.

Firstly, moulds were taken of the damaged features and these were then used to form replacement terracotta.

To repair damaged or altered facings, brickwork reclaimed from the demolition of an adjoining building was used. The building’s roof was also reslated and insulated.

Internally, all plaster was removed to allow the building to dry out. New plaster was then applied to the walls, with new plasterboard ceilings fitted.

One problem was the suspended ground floor. Its structural steelwork was badly corroded for one third of its length. This section had to be replaced, with the rest of the floor stabilised with new structural support.

Work to the chapel included refurbishing antique leaded windows and replacing a rather unusual timber shingles feature to the chapel spire. Dormer windows were also replaced with an extractor system.

Internally, a partition was removed to create a 4,550 sq ft performing area with 216 seats.

Construction began in January 1997 and was completed in December.
