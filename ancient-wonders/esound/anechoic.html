Anechoic Rooms
As part of the ongoing research and development required of testing there can often be a need for companies to carry out accurate measurements of noise emissions.

This may be from the engine or associated equipment or in the general vehicle noise.

Simulation of 'free field' environments can only be achieved by the use of a special anechoic or semi-anechoic chamber. In effect to achieve a room with at least 99% absorption at predetermined frequencies e.g. 100HZ.









 


Envirosound have developed a standard acoustic wedge design manufactured from a non-inflammable lightweight rigid foam material which can be supplied in modular banks and adjusted to suit differing 'cut off' frequencies. The design and install of anechoic facilities can be readily carried out by Envirosound engineers with careful consideration to optimum room sizes, base structure design, actual cut off frequencies required and high performance attenuated ventilation systems.

For the control of fuel and combustion vapour a low level purge extraction system is provided. This is either an addition to the cell extract or a separate system. A control interlock is advisable to run the purge prior to the engine start up.

To provide comfort conditioning for the control room area, we normally fit a concealed split air conditioning unit. It re-circulates air around the room through either the ceiling grid or diffusers. Room temperature control is via an adjustable wall mounted thermostat. Ventilation systems for gas analyser equipment for both heat, waste, product extraction and bottle stores are available.
