AB Testing Mistakes not to make
Believing everything you read
Just because a Purple button gets a 50% greater click rate than a green button doesnâ€™t mean itâ€™s 50% better.
If conversion rates at the next step are 75% fewer than the green button, then it is not really improving matters.
Using other peoples results
Another website cites an improvement in Purple button over green button
This doesnâ€™t mean the same thing will happen to your site. You may have a lot of different or additional factors which affect the results.
Ending Tests before they are significant
End tests too early and you may as well not bother testing at all.
Running too many tests in too short time.
Related to the above, but also a lot of tests could result in more losing tests which could lose you click-throughs/money. A lot of tests over a long time period is fine, but the need time to become significant.
Testing too many variables at a time
Test too many variables at the same time and it becomes more difficult to judge what is working and what is not. Changing a video / headline / CTA colour and form at the same time (without any actual radical changes) and you wonâ€™t know which variable was having what effect.
Not trying again
Your first tests mail not achieve statistically significant results, you have to keep on trying. May be an indication to try something more fundamental.
Effective A/B testing focuses on continual improvement, as long as you are improving you are going in the right direction.
Expecting big wins from small changes
if you are getting big wins from small changes, there is probably something else going on you site.
Wins are often proportional to the nature of the change.
Maximise potential of big changes first, then concentrate on smaller ones.
Diving right in
This can be a mistake.
Better to conduct some kind of survey first to establish the big wins
