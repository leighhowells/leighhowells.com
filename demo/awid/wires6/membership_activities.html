AWID Membership Mobilized via Social Media in Unprecedented Numbers

during CSW58!

 

For this year’s 58thsession of the Commission on the Status of Women (CSW 58), AWID took its mobilization to the internet, sending a strong message that women’s rights needs to be at the core of the new development agenda!

 

On March 10 and 11, AWID members, partners and allies from over 50 countries joined our social media mobilization reaching 1.7 million people through our twitterthon and resulting in 57,588 views of our 16 Facebook posts! Take a look at our infographic to learn more and visit our CSW 58 special focus section. 

 

 

Webinar on Women’s Rights in the Post-2015 Development Agenda

 

On March 19th AWID hosted a webinar on Women’s Rights in the Post-2015 Development Agenda for AWID and Young Feminist Wire members.The session focused on Post-2015 and the Sustainable Development goals processes, as well as discussed the challenges and opportunities for women's rights organizations around the world to influence the new development agenda.

 

Presenters at the session included Bhumika Muchhala (Third World Network) and Bridget Burns (Women’s Environment and Development Organization).

Beyond Investing in Women and Girls

27 February 2014

 

AWID’s three-part compendium of new research provides an in-depth analysis of the current funding trends and actors impacting women’s rights organizing; the financial status of women’s organizations around the world; and the collective impact of women’s rights organizations, when supported in meaningful and strategic ways, to build women’s collective power for change to advance women’s rights.

CSW58: Too much Time Spent Pushing Back

 

This year's negotiations at CSW 58 saw women's rights advocates, organisations and movements working extremely hard to retain existing human rights language, obligations and commitments, and attempting to push forward language that contained clear commitments for gender equality and women's human rights; but present also, were strong fundamentalist forces pushing back on any, and all, advancement of rights language.
