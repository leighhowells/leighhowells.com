Responsive Web Design

1650 to 1920

With only 11.5% of users using the largest range of screen resolutions, fixed width sites can find themselves swimming in lots of redundant screen space. This is often not such a bad thing, it focuses the content area. Worse, however, are sites set to use a fluid 100% screen width, as they can end up with very long line lengths, which is bad for readability. Using media queries there is an opportunity to deliver an enhanced experience for these power users.

1280 to 1650

Resolutions on widescreen displays and larger monitors now accounts for 16% of the global audience. There are opportunities to find creative techniques to make the most of the extra real estate available. For example on the Glasgow University site images are used outside of the main content area, enriching the visual experience but they are not vital to the use of the site.

1152 to 1280

This range of screen widths is becoming ever more prolific beyond 1024x768, especially amongst lower end LCD panels and widescreen displays. A fixed width or max-width 960px fluid site sits comfortably in a frame at these widths.

1024 to 1152

In late 2011, 1024x768 is still a very commonly viewed resolution, with 14% of worldwide users. We still generally build site to cater for this fairly low common denominator.

800 to 1024

Up to 1024 pixels wide, your users are very likely to be using a tablet computer, such as an iPad or a higher specification smartphone. Old Desktop machines will be forced to horizontal scroll a standard 960px website.

640 to 800

In 2011 users in this range are likely to be using a mobile devices, so consideration for touch screen devices is vital. There is a chance that very old Desktop or netbook users are trying to access your site, how will your site best cater these? See how the EDF site adapts to give a 'best fit' to this size.

480 to 640

The domain of many tablet devices and higher performance smartphones. Thought should be given to touch screens users.


For a more 'Responsive' Christmas, please update your browser!
